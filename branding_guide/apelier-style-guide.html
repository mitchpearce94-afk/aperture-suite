01
Brand Overview

Apelier is an all-in-one photography business platform. The name blends aperture (the heart of every lens) with atelier (a creative workshop), positioning the product as the place where photographers do their best work — from shoot to delivery.

The brand voice is premium but approachable, confident but not loud. We speak to professional photographers who value craft, efficiency, and a beautiful client experience.

Tagline: Shoot. Edit. Deliver.

Website: apelier.com.au

02
Logo

The Apelier logo consists of two elements: the aperture mark (a six-blade aperture icon with a ghost camera silhouette) and the wordmark (set in Libre Baskerville). These always appear together in the primary lockup. The mark may be used standalone as a favicon or app icon.

✓ Do

Use the logo on cream (#FAF9F7) or dark (#0E0E10) backgrounds. Keep the aperture mark and wordmark together. Use approved colour variants only.

Don't rotate, stretch, or skew the logo. Don't change the blade colours. Don't place on busy photographic backgrounds without sufficient contrast. Don't remove the ghost camera from the mark.

Typography

Two typefaces form the brand's typographic system. Libre Baskerville is the display face — used for the wordmark, headings, and anywhere the brand needs to feel premium and editorial. Manrope is the utility face — used for taglines, navigation, labels, and UI elements.

05
Voice & Tone

Premium but not pretentious. We respect photographers' craft and time. We don't oversell or use hype language.

Confident but concise. We state what the product does clearly. No fluff, no jargon walls.

Warm but professional. Like talking to a colleague who's really good at their job — helpful, direct, and never condescending.

We say: "Your gallery is ready" · "247 photos, delivered" · "From session to client in minutes"

We don't say: "Revolutionary AI-powered solution" · "Disrupt your workflow" · "Unlock your potential"

06
Imagery & Photography

Marketing imagery should feel natural, warm, and aspirational. Think golden hour, natural light, candid moments. Avoid overly saturated colours, heavy filters, or stock photo aesthetics.

When showing the product in context, use clean device mockups on cream or dark backgrounds. Never overlay the UI on busy photographs.

Photo watermark: The aperture mark + "Apelier" wordmark in white at 18% opacity, bottom-centre of images.
