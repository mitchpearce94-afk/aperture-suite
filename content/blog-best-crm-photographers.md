## What Photographers Actually Need From a CRM

Before comparing tools, it's worth being clear about what the job actually requires. A good photography CRM needs to:

- **Capture leads and track them through the booking process** — from first inquiry to signed contract to deposit paid
- **Send and receive contracts digitally** — e-sign, legally binding, no printing
- **Create and send invoices** — with deposit/final payment splits, and ideally automatic reminders
- **Manage the calendar** — see all your jobs, block dates, avoid double-bookings
- **Automate the repetitive stuff** — booking confirmations, contract reminders, invoice follow-ups, gallery delivery notifications
- **Connect to everything else** — ideally, the CRM should talk to your gallery software so you're not manually copying client details twice

Most CRMs nail some of these. Few nail all of them.

## Studio Ninja

**Price:** ~$25-35/mo AUD  
**Best for:** Photographers who've been using it for years and haven't had a reason to switch

Studio Ninja is still the default recommendation among Australian wedding photographers, but that reputation was earned a few years ago. Post-ImageQuix acquisition, the product has stalled. Development has slowed dramatically, support response times have stretched to 5+ days, and features that were promised publicly are still sitting in the queue.

It does the basics well — lead management, contracts, invoices, calendar. But "the basics" is about all you're getting now. No gallery integration. No AI editing. No meaningful automation beyond what was there two years ago.

If you're already on Studio Ninja and things are working, there's no urgent reason to move. If you're starting from scratch or actively frustrated, there are better options.

**Verdict:** Solid foundation, but standing still while the market moves.

---

## HoneyBook

**Price:** ~$39-79/mo AUD  
**Best for:** Photographers who also run other creative services (video, design, etc.)

HoneyBook is a polished, actively-developed tool. The interface is clean, the automation is genuinely good, and it keeps shipping new features. The problem is it's not built for photographers specifically — it's built for creative service businesses broadly, which means it covers a lot of ground and doesn't go deep on any of it.

You won't find gallery delivery, RAW file handling, or anything photography-specific in HoneyBook. It's a CRM and contract tool that works fine for photographers but doesn't understand your workflow the way a photography-specific tool does.

The other issue: HoneyBook raised their prices significantly in 2025. Photographers with lower booking volumes started questioning whether the value was there.

**Verdict:** Good tool, not built for you, and getting expensive.

---

## Dubsado

**Price:** ~$24/mo AUD  
**Best for:** Budget-conscious photographers who don't mind a steep setup curve

Dubsado is the cheap option, and if you're willing to put in the work upfront, it can be configured to do a lot. The automation workflow builder is powerful. You can build almost any process you want.

The catch: "you can build almost any process you want" means you have to build it yourself. Dubsado is not opinionated — it doesn't come pre-set for photographers, so you'll spend days setting up templates, workflows, and sequences before you get any value from it.

It also looks dated. That's a minor complaint, but when you're sending proposals to clients billing $5,000+ for a wedding, the presentation matters.

**Verdict:** Best price-to-power ratio if you're patient. Not a great out-of-the-box experience.

---

## Sprout Studio

**Price:** ~$59-99/mo AUD  
**Best for:** Photographers who want CRM + gallery delivery without paying for two separate tools

Sprout Studio is the most direct Studio Ninja competitor — it combines CRM, contracts, invoicing, and gallery delivery in one platform. On paper, that's compelling. In practice, the execution is mixed.

Users frequently report that while the breadth is impressive, the depth isn't quite there. Things that should be simple take too many clicks. The reliability has been questioned in community forums. But it's actively developed and the concept is right — combining CRM and gallery delivery is the direction the market needs to go.

**Verdict:** Right idea, still finding its footing. Worth watching.
